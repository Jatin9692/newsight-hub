President Donald Trump announced a headline US–China tariff truce and teased a major US–India trade deal, sending global markets and Indian exporters surging on hopes of tariff relief and new opportunities.

1. Background: Anatomy of the US–China Trade Truce

After nearly three years of tit-for-tat tariffs—up to 45% on over $500 billion in goods—Trump said the U.S. “has just signed” a deal with China based on April’s Geneva talks. However, no detailed rollback schedules or enforcement rules were published.

  • No tariff schedule or product list released
  • Absence of dispute-resolution framework
  • Seen as a political ceasefire rather than a full treaty

FAQs: US–China Trade Deal

What is the US–China trade deal?

A temporary truce halting new tariffs between the U.S. and China, pausing 25–45% duties without full rollback terms.

Why did they make this deal?

To calm global markets and avoid further economic disruption from high tariffs.

What did Trump announce today?

He announced a US–China tariff truce and a forthcoming, major US–India trade deal.

Is the tariff war over?

No—existing duties remain; this truce only pauses future hikes.

When will full terms be released?

Draft annexes are expected by mid-July 2025.

3. Trump’s “Very Big” US–India Trade Deal

Immediately after the China news, Trump promised a “very big” deal with India. Likely focus areas include textiles ($40B exports), pharmaceuticals ($13B), and IT services ($150B).

  • Textiles & apparel: seeking phased U.S. duty cuts
  • Pharmaceuticals: pushing for faster FDA approvals
  • IT services: lobbying for streamlined H-1B visas

FAQs: US–India Trade Deal

What sectors benefit most?

Textiles, pharmaceuticals, and IT services stand to gain significantly.

What is this deal?

An agreement to reduce tariffs and regulatory hurdles on Indian exports.

When will talks begin?

Around the G20 summit in New Delhi, September 2025.

How large is US–India trade?

About $120B annually, with room for expansion.

Why is it important for India?

It supports job creation and deepens strategic ties.
